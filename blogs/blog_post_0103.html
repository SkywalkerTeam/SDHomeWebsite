Prediction...Prediction?

The unemployment rate in the US will continue to improve slowly as the government continues to make business friendly policy decisions.
The unemployment rate in Austin will likely hold steady at around 7% as the local job market will not be able to outpace the number of families moving to Austin.
As the economy shows signs of recovery, the Federal Reserve will allow interest rates to rise slowly, which will cause mortgage rates to rise to the 6% range.
The number of home sales in Austin will improve between 10% and 20% compared with 2010 as many buyers decide to get off the fence as they realize interest rates have already bottomed.
Home prices in Austin will hold steady or improve a few percentage points, but will most likely not decline. The number of foreclosures on the market will prevent home prices in Austin from making any significant gains.
The rate of foreclosure in the Austin area will likely plateau around current levels, with the highest foreclosure rates in suburban communities like Kyle, Hutto, Pflugerville and Manor.
Home builders will regain their confidence in the Austin market and will start aggressively building spec homes, but only in the neighborhoods that are relatively close to the city.
Home sales in the $1,000,000+ price range will start to improve when potential buyers for these homes start to feel more confidence as their stock portfolio and business performance improve.
Oil prices will continue to rise as the demand for oil increases worldwide. I am already hearing from Houstonian's who are back in the market to buy second homes on the lake or luxury condos in downtown Austin.
